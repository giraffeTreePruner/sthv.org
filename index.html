What are Strong Towns?

Strong Towns is a non-profit organization that aims to support a model of growth that allows America's towns to become financially strong places to live for all. We are the Happy Valley local group, aiming to help State College and the surrounding area to be a better and more resilient place. Some of our key aims are:

  • Enable sustainable growth by promoting “missing middle housing” to increase affordability, accessibility and choice.
  • Increase accessibility and variety of transportation options, reducing the need to rely on cars, and reducing traffic.
  • Change zoning laws to allow for more mixed-use neighborhoods, increasing profitability and liveability
  • Eliminating parking minimums so business don't have to devote such a large portion of their value to low use land.

Currently we are lobbying the State College City Council to not demolish local businesses (Canyon Pizza, Music Mart, The brewery and Bottle Shop) to build a parking lot ( petition ). Success! Town hall recently voted to repeal their use of eminent domain on this lot. They have not yet decided where the new parking structure will go. We suggest moving this project so that it is not in the middle of downtown.

Another local issue on our docket is the lack of property reassessment. Many plots in State College have not had their value assessed since 1994.
